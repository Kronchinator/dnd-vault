# Goatius's Stone

*A smooth grey river stone, slightly too warm to the touch for something carried by a construct.*

## Description

An unremarkable stone — the kind found on any riverbank. Small enough to fit in a closed fist. Perfectly smooth, the colour of ash. Melogg has had it for as long as he can remember, though he cannot say where it came from.

It is warm. Not hot — just warm, like it has been sitting in a patch of sun. It does not cool.

On the underside, barely visible, is a small rune that Melogg did not carve.

## Mechanical Effect

Once per long rest, Melogg can close his fist around the stone as a **bonus action**. He gains **temporary HP equal to his Constitution modifier (3)**. These last until his next long rest.

## Roleplay Notes

- Melogg finds it grounding in a way he cannot explain. When the world is too loud or too much, he holds it.
- He does not know the rune. If anyone in the party reads it, the Arcana DC is 18 — and even on a success, they recognise the script but not the meaning. It is very old.
- The stone grows slightly warmer when [[Goatius Everest]] is within 30 feet.

## DM Secret

[[Goatius Everest]] gave Melogg this stone deliberately. It is a tracking anchor, keeping the 100-metre tether stable. The rune is Goatius's mark. The warmth is Goatius's presence, always close.

The stone is not the same thing as [[Matthew]]'s fragment inside Melogg, but the two may resonate. If Melogg is badly harmed, dies, or tries to move beyond the tether, the stone may heat sharply, crack, pulse with red-black light, or briefly transmit Matthew's voice.

When the truth of the tether is eventually revealed, this stone should be the moment Melogg realises it predates anything he thought he knew about himself.
