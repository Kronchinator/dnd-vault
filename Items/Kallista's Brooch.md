# Kallista's Brooch

*A small pressed flower — a pale mushroom bloom — preserved in resin and set in a simple silver clasp. [[Princess Kallista]] wore it every day.*

## Description
[[Princess Kallista]] had a hyperfixation with mushrooms. This brooch was the physical proof of it — a tiny pale bloom she found in the woods as a girl, pressed flat and sealed in resin by a castle jeweller as a joke gift. She wore it on everything. She was wearing it the day she died.

Amoris has it. She doesn't talk about it.

## Mechanical Effect
**The brooch is the one thing that never changes.**

When Amoris uses her Shapechanger ability to shift form, the brooch always appears — on whatever she's wearing, in the same position, unchanged. It cannot be shifted away. It cannot be hidden by her ability. Even if she wills it, the brooch remains.

Once per long rest, Amoris can touch the brooch and **regain one expended 1st-level spell slot**. When she does, the flower inside briefly blooms — colour returning to it for just a moment before fading back to pale.

## Roleplay Notes
- The brooch is a liability as much as a comfort — it is a fixed point on a changeling who survives by having none
- Anyone who has seen the brooch before can identify Amoris by it regardless of her current form
- If Amoris ever loses it or has it taken, treat it as a significant emotional moment
- The blooming effect is visible to anyone watching — it lasts only a second, but it is beautiful and strange

## DM Secret
Kallista was wearing the brooch when she touched the rune. The brooch absorbed a trace of the curse — which is why it cannot be shifted away and why it responds to Amoris's magic. It is the only physical object in the world that carries Kallista's last moment.

In Act 3, this may become relevant to the ritual. The brooch is not just a keepsake.
