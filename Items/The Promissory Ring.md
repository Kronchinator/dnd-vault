# The Promissory Ring

*A plain silver band engraved on the inside with names too small to read without magnification. It belonged to Damius Rooflane.*

## Description
Unassuming from the outside — a simple silver ring, slightly tarnished, no gemstone, no crest. The kind of ring a person wears every day for thirty years without thinking about it.

Turn it inward and the interior is covered in names, etched in a hand so fine it requires a magnifying glass to read. Dozens of names. Some are crossed out. Some have small symbols beside them — debts settled, debts outstanding.

[[Damius Rooflane]] left it to Aeritch without explanation. She has never been able to read all the names.

## Mechanical Effect
While wearing this ring, the wearer gains **advantage on Charisma (Deception) checks when making a promise they genuinely intend to keep.**

If the wearer makes a promise they do not intend to keep while wearing the ring, it **tightens** — not painfully, but unmistakably. Like a warning. The ring knows.

## Roleplay Notes
- Aeritch may not fully understand the mechanic at first — let her discover it through play
- The ring does not force honesty; it simply has a preference
- It's a good prop for negotiation scenes — the party can see Aeritch pause before putting it on
- Names inside include at least two that Aeritch recognises from her network of favours

## DM Secret
One of the crossed-out names inside the ring is [[Anakis]]'s. [[Damius Rooflane]] and Anakis knew each other — the nature of that relationship is a thread for later.

Another name, not crossed out, belongs to someone still alive and still owed something. When Aeritch finally reads all the names, she will inherit more than a ring.
