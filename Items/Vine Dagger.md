# Vine Dagger

*A dagger with green vine and rose etchings running along the hilt and the flat of the blade. It's too pretty to be a market stall dagger. It cost much less than it should have.*

## Description
Not obviously magical. The etchings are detailed — vines wound tight around the handle, small roses in bloom at intervals, the same pattern continuing faintly up the blade as though it grew there. The craftsmanship is well above what you'd expect from a market stall.

[[Amoris Scilia]] bought it at a market stall in Aldenmire, still wearing [[Lady Timbra]]'s form. The stall owner mentioned she'd acquired it recently from a man who'd sold it too cheaply — didn't want it anymore, or needed coin, or both. The stall owner seemed faintly aware that her customer was not quite what she appeared.

## Mechanical Notes
- Standard dagger statistics: 1d4 piercing, finesse, light, thrown (range 20/60)
- No confirmed magical properties yet

## Roleplay Notes
- The stall owner knew something was off about "Lady Timbra" — whether she knew she was talking to a changeling, or just that the old woman felt wrong, is a thread
- The previous owner sold it well below value — why didn't he want it?
- The etchings are too consistent to be decorative. Someone put intention into this dagger.
