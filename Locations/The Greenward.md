# The Greenward

## Description
A wide common green on the north edge of Aldenmire where livestock used to graze. Mostly abandoned for two months. The grass grows wrong here — dark at the stem, almost black, curling inward where it should stand upright. The blossoms that push through are pale, scentless, and cold to the touch.

The ground smells like turned earth after rain, except there has been no rain.

Children have been told to stay away. Most adults avoid it without discussing why.

## Notable Features
- **The Black Stems** — The discolouration begins at ground level and travels upward. The tips of the grass are still green, almost luminous. The effect is wrong in a way that's hard to articulate.
- **The Root Geometry** — A DC 13 Nature or Religion check reveals the roots beneath the dark patches spiral downward in a perfect geometric pattern. This is not decay. Something beneath is pulling them deliberately.
- **The Spiral Centre** — At the geometric centre of the Greenward, the earth is slightly sunken, as though something heavy stood there repeatedly. Grass here is completely black and dead.

## The Faceless Figure
Three weeks before the party arrives, [[Maret]] observed a figure crossing the Greenward at midnight. It cast no shadow under a full moon. It walked to the spiral centre, stood for several minutes, and disappeared when she blinked. Where it stood, the grass was black by morning.

**DM Secret:** The figure was a branded changeling — a person with the same rune mark as Amoris, drawn to this location by the pull of the seal beneath. They are not a villain. They are frightened. They can be found later.

## DM Notes
- The Greenward sits above one of the seal's resonance points — a place where the imprisoned god's influence bleeds through
- The black growth will worsen over time if the party does not act; by Session 3 it should be spreading into the edge of town
- The spiral centre is a safe location for ritual investigation but dangerous for extended rest — creatures resting here have disturbed dreams
- Do not explain the connection to the seal in Act 1; let it be unsettling and mysterious
