# The Old Chord

## Description
A small, half-ruined shrine on the north edge of Aldenmire, set back from the road behind an overgrown hedge. Most locals assume it is decorative — a remnant of old craftsmanship, nothing more. Nobody maintains it. Nobody quite remembers when it was built.

The stonework is old enough that the style matches no current tradition. The carvings are musical — instruments depicted in relief across every surface: lutes, flutes, drums, lyres. No prayers. No god's name. Only music.

One wall has collapsed inward. The remaining three walls form a partial enclosure around a flat central stone, like an altar that was never used for sacrifice.

## Notable Features
- **The Carvings** — Instruments depicted with unusual precision. Some of the carved instruments match the Ravendeer family instruments exactly, including a lyre with the same curl of the tuning pegs as the Lyre of Veilsong.
- **The Central Stone** — Flat, waist-height, worn smooth in the centre. Three shallow indentations are carved into the surface in a triangular arrangement. They are the right size and shape to hold: a lyre, a flute, and a drum.
- **The Resonance** — When the Lyre of Veilsong is brought within 30 feet of the shrine, it hums audibly. The hum matches no note in any known scale.

## DM Notes
- The three indentations in the central stone are where the Lyre of Veilsong, the Flute of Stillwater, and the Drum of Rootsong belong — they are the three components of the Seal of Thoria
- Do not let the party fully investigate this in Session 1; describe it in passing, note the Lyre's hum, and let it sit
- In Act 2, this shrine becomes a major lore location — records of the original seal-keepers can be found in the collapsed section if excavated
- The shrine predates Aldenmire itself; the town was built around it without knowing why people kept settling here
