# Zotseudan

## Player-Facing Version

Zotseudan is the kingdom Melogg believes he came from.

It is a land of fertile fields and steady prosperity. No one in Zotseudan lived in poverty, yet no one possessed excessive wealth either. Its people lived simple, comfortable lives, content with what they had.

Melogg remembers being abandoned as an infant at **The House of Saint Edith**, an orphanage where he grew up among children he considered brothers and sisters. He also remembers being banished from Zotseudan after a witch's curse transformed him into a living golem.

---

## Important Places

- **The House of Saint Edith** — the orphanage where Melogg believes he was raised.
- The forests beyond the kingdom — where Melogg wandered after his exile.

---

## DM Secret

Zotseudan should remain unresolved for now.

It may be:

1. A real kingdom that Melogg truly came from before being altered.
2. A real kingdom used as source material for a false memory.
3. A fabricated memory-structure created to stabilize Melogg's identity.
4. A place that exists, but not in the way Melogg remembers it.

Do not decide too early unless the campaign needs the answer. The uncertainty is useful.

---

## Reveal Hooks

- No one has heard of Zotseudan, or everyone has heard contradictory versions.
- A map shows Zotseudan, but not where Melogg remembers it.
- The House of Saint Edith exists but has no record of Melogg.
- Someone recognizes Melogg's story as a children's cautionary tale rather than history.
- An old bounty notice describes a monster encounter similar to Melogg's memory, but the details are wrong.
