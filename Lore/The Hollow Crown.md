# The Hollow Crown

> ⚠️ **DM-ONLY. This is the spine of the campaign.** Almost none of this is known to the party in Act 1. Read [[The Seal of Thoria]] alongside this.

## The one-sentence version

Every member of the party has been **made, unmade, or emptied out** by the same machine — and that machine is a crown nobody wears on their head: a role that fills a person, hollows them out, and uses them to hold the world together. The villains are not trying to conquer anything. They are trying to **put the crown down** — and they cannot do it without making monsters of themselves.

---

## What is imprisoned: The Root of Thoria

*(aka the Green Beneath, the First Root, the Unending Green)*

Beneath Thoria is not a demon or a dark god in the usual sense. It is **the first living thing** — a primordial growth that predates kingdoms, names, and the separation of one creature from another. It is nature with the mercy taken out: the horror of growth that never stops. Left unchecked, it pulls all separate, distinct living things back down into one undifferentiated green mass. Roots through skin. Bark over faces. Spirals in the grass that are too perfect to be accident.

This is the "very sinister, nature-related magic" the campaign runs on. It is why:
- [[Princess Kallista]] died with **blackened roots taking every inch of her skin**.
- [[Melogg]]'s cursed body sprouts moss and flowers from the cracks — the Root grows through him.
- [[The Greenward]] grows in deliberate geometric spirals: *something beneath is pulling them.*
- [[Ressi Res Essiressires|Ressi]]'s tattoos are roots, vines, watchful eyes, and warding script.

The [[The Seal of Thoria|Seal of Thoria]] holds the Root down. But a seal of this scale cannot be held by stone and song alone. It needs a **living keystone**.

## The Hollow Crown (the role)

The Hollow Crown is that keystone. It is not an object worn on the brow — it is a burden that **fills a person and empties them in exchange**. To be Crowned is to become the container that holds the Root down *from the inside.* You stop being entirely a person and slowly become a function. Hence **Hollow**: to wear it is to be hollowed out.

The cruelty is structural, and it generates the entire villain:

> **You cannot put the Crown down.** Setting it aside means releasing what it contains. The only way to stop holding it is to **pass it to a new vessel** — and the transfer requires a living being capable of bearing the Root's weight, which almost no one is.

So the bearer faces a choice with no clean answer: hold it forever and be slowly erased, or find/build/break a successor and damn someone else. Centuries of bearers have chosen the second. That is the rot under everything in Thoria.

### Kael is the thesis

[[Kael Vesperis|Kael]]'s entire character is the campaign's moral question made flesh. His order taught him that divinity *"is not a crown — it is a burden to carry carefully."* His casting produces a **fractured ember halo, not a perfect ring** — a hollow crown of light. His ideal is redemption; his bond is carrying the people he could not save. The central question of the campaign is the one Kael was built to ask: **Can the Crown be ended instead of passed on?** Every antagonist says no. Kael is the reason the party might find a third answer — or the reason a hero volunteers to be the next vessel.

## The villains

### The Gilded King (the decoy BBEG)

*Working name: **Aurel**. Known to cults and servants as **the Gilded King**, **the Gardener in Gold**, or simply **the Architect**. Use whichever the table latches onto.*

This is the "eventual BBEG" already seeded in [[Ressi Res Essiressires|Ressi]]'s file: the one who found the **Painted Hollow**, destroyed/harvested it, and built Ressi from its remains. Present him for most of the campaign as a classic dark sorcerer — a man who **makes people**. Let the party hate him and hunt him.

**The truth (Reveal #1 — "he's not a conqueror, he's a man begging to die"):**
The Gilded King is the **previous Hollow Crown**. He has been emptied for centuries. Everything monstrous he has done — razing the Painted Hollow, building [[Ressi Res Essiressires|Ressi]], steering the [[Alberich Clan]], engineering deaths — was an attempt to **manufacture a replacement so he can finally stop.** Ressi is his proof-of-concept: evidence that a person can be *built* to channel power that should require faith, inheritance, or birthright. He wants her back as "proof and key."

When the party finally corners him, he is not triumphant. He is **relieved**. And defeating him changes nothing — because he already succeeded in passing the Crown on, years ago, by disguising the transfer as an accident: a cursed trunk, a rune, in the woods outside [[Ravencroft]].

#### The gold motif (use this everywhere)

The Crown's power, when it bleeds out, looks like **gilding** — golden light that hardens into amber/resin. It preserves and it binds. This is the visual signature that threads the whole campaign, and it lets you foreshadow without saying a word:
- [[Corvin Fels|Corvin]]'s "Gilded Binding" in [[Session 02 — Standard Procedure]] is the Architect's gift — the party's **first contact** with the Crown's currency, long before they understand it.
- [[Kallista's Brooch]] — a mushroom preserved in **resin** — is amber, the Crown's medium. It is "the only object that carries the moment of her death" *because amber is what the Crown uses to preserve.*
- [[Amoris Scilia|Amoris]]'s brand **aches and flares** in the presence of gilded magic — same source.

### Kallista (the real final antagonist)

**Reveal #2 — the WTF, and the heart-wrenching one.** The dead best friend [[Amoris Scilia|Amoris]] has spent the entire campaign grieving and avenging **is the new vessel.**

The Gilded King chose [[Princess Kallista]] precisely because she was the **Astrologer of [[Ravencroft]]** — the one trained to "bridge the constellations and the earth," the perfect interface between the Root below and the world above. The cursed trunk in the woods was a **transfer ritual** disguised as a trap. The rune did not kill Kallista; it **took** her. Amoris lunging to pull her free is why the transfer half-failed and the brand split onto Amoris's own hand.

Kallista is the figure stirring beneath [[The Greenward]] and, eventually, beneath Thoria itself. Crucially:
- **Early campaign:** she is still mostly herself — frightened, grieving, half-aware, reaching out (dreams, the brand, the brooch).
- **By the climax:** she is nearly hollow. What speaks may wear her face and her memories and still not quite be her.

Amoris set out to **avenge Kallista's murder** and discovers that the thing she has to stop — and maybe kill, again — *is* Kallista. "I came to avenge you." / "You already did. You just didn't know what you were avenging."

Both villains are people you ache for. The Architect was a victim who became a monster trying to escape an unbearable duty. Kallista never chose any of it and is being erased in real time.

## How each wound plugs in

**Core spine threads (link tightly):**
- **[[Amoris Scilia|Amoris]]** — brand = transfer-spillover. Her arc terminates at Kallista. Her changeling nature (hollow, then filled with another's shape) is the Crown in miniature.
- **[[Ressi Res Essiressires|Ressi]]** — the Architect's prototype replacement-Crown. "Proof his method works." [[Ressi Res Essiressires|Tigger]] carries a surviving Painted Hollow soul-fragment. Existing reveal *"You are what survived"* slots in unchanged.
- **[[Aeritch Ravendeer|Aeritch]]** — the Ravendeer massacre was **not** about trade. A strong seal won't let the Crown migrate; you must **weaken** it first. The instruments maintain the seal, so the Ashwing's employer-above-the-Alberich wanted them seized/destroyed to force the transfer. Her family died as a step in the Architect's escape plan. The [[Lyre of Veilsong]] survived the fire untouched because the seal protects its own components. [[Damius Rooflane]] "knew more than he said" because he was researching the Crown.
- **[[Kael Vesperis|Kael]]** — the **Ashbound Canticle** was a faith order that historically protected (or supplied) Crown-bearers; "scattered by conflict" because someone wanted them out of the way. His fractured halo is a hollow crown of light. He may be the *ideal* vessel the Crown is reaching for — which is why events keep pulling him toward the center.

**Parallel / rhyming threads (don't force the link):**
- **[[Melogg]] & [[Matthew]]** — keep this its own engine, but let it *rhyme*: Matthew is a being desperate to **resign from an eternal duty**, manufacturing a failsafe to escape it. He is the comic mirror of the Architect. **Optional late knot:** the [[The Ashen Desk|Ashen Desk]] is the cosmic office that *administers the Crown* — Matthew has been trying to quit the same bureaucracy that issues the burden. Hold in reserve; only pull it if the table wants total convergence.
- **[[John Jackbox|John]]** — the "valuable artifact" the Alberich sent him to fetch (then betrayed him over) is a seal-relic or instrument fragment. Bonus tragedy: the poverty and extraction he hates is **downstream of the land being emptied** (the Greenward writ large). The rot he wants to overthrow has a metaphysical root — and he was tricked into helping dig it up.
- **[[Anakis]]** — blank slate; name crossed out on [[The Promissory Ring]]. **Recommended:** she is the Ashwing's "runaway noble bride," secretly tying Aeritch's savior to Aeritch's enemy (a late grenade). **Alternative:** she is a Crown candidate who *escaped* the Architect — the one that got away, which is why he had to build Ressi instead.

The elegant through-line: **everyone at the table carries the same wound in a different shape** — built, branded, cursed-with-growth, scarred-with-fractured-divinity, robbed-of-the-instruments, used-to-fetch-the-key. They do not need linked backstories. They need to slowly realize they are all survivors of the same machine.

## The reveal cascade (pacing)

1. **Act 1 — the Alberich are the villains.** Greedy merchants; [[Corvin Fels|Corvin]] the cold collector; [[The Ashwing]] the muscle. Corvin's gilded magic is the only crack of something larger. *Explain nothing.*
2. **Act 2 — a bigger hand appears.** Someone wants the instruments, wants Ressi, "makes people." The party builds a mental image of a dark sorcerer and starts hunting the Gilded King. **This is the bait.** Plant [[Oswin Dray|Oswin]] as a mid-act "is it him?" red herring (see below) to keep them off Kallista's scent.
3. **Act 2→3 — Reveal #1.** They corner the Architect. He is relieved, not victorious. The Crown has already moved on. Killing him is not a win.
4. **Act 3 — Reveal #2.** The new vessel is Kallista. Amoris's revenge becomes a tragedy. The party must decide whether to end the Crown, pass it on, or have one of their own take it.

### The Oswin red herring
The warmest, safest NPC — the man they protected in Session 1 — makes a perfect false lead. His town **sits on a resonance point**; his mother warned him off the north edge; he is bone-tired in a way that reads as supernatural once the party is primed. Let them suspect Oswin is the current Crown for a stretch in Act 2. **Optional deepening:** Oswin really *is* a **failed earlier vessel** — the Crown tried him, he couldn't hold it, and he was left half-emptied, which is why he stays over the wound and why he is so weary. This makes the red herring *true enough to hurt* without being the final answer.

## Naming & open design questions

- **Entity:** *The Root of Thoria* (formal) / *the Green Beneath* (colloquial). Swap freely.
- **Role:** *the Hollow Crown*; a bearer is *Crowned* / *the Hollow King or Queen*.
- **Architect:** *Aurel, the Gilded King.* Lock the name only when the party is close to meeting him.
- Can the Crown truly be **ended**, or only moved? (Decide before Act 3 — it determines whether the ending is hopeful, sacrificial, or bittersweet.)
- Are destroyed instruments restorable? (Ties to [[Aeritch Ravendeer|Aeritch]]'s arc and the [[Flute of Stillwater]] / [[Drum of Rootsong]] husks.)
- How much of Kallista remains at the climax? (Controls how brutal Amoris's final choice is.)
