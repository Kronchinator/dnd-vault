# The Seal of Thoria

## Overview
Something is imprisoned beneath [[Thoria]]: **[[The Hollow Crown|The Root of Thoria]]**, the first living thing — nature with the mercy taken out, a growth that wants to pull all separate creatures back into one green mass. The Seal that holds it is older than [[Aldenmire]], older than any current religious tradition. Its influence bleeds through resonance points where the earth is thin.

The party does not know any of this yet. **Do not explain it in Act 1.**

## Two parts to the Seal
The Seal is held by **stone and song** *and* by a **living keystone**:

1. **The Components (stone and song)** — the three [[Ravendeer Clan]] instruments, anchored at [[The Old Chord]] shrine. These do the structural work.
2. **The Living Keystone (the bearer)** — a person who holds the Root down *from the inside.* This role is the **[[The Hollow Crown|Hollow Crown]]**, and it is the heart of the campaign. See that file.

A seal this large cannot hold on instruments alone. Weaken the instruments and the seal strains — but it is the **bearer** who actually contains the thing.

## Why weakening the Seal matters (the transfer mechanic)
A **strong** seal will not let the Crown migrate from one vessel to another — the Root is held too tightly to move. To **transfer** the Crown to a new bearer, the seal must first be **weakened** so the Root can stir enough to cross over.

This reframes [[Aeritch Ravendeer|Aeritch]]'s tragedy: the [[Ravendeer Clan]] massacre was never about trade routes. Someone needed the instruments destroyed or seized to weaken the seal — to make a transfer possible. Two of three instruments are now ruined. The seal is at its weakest in living memory, which is exactly the condition a desperate bearer would engineer to escape the Crown.

## The Resonance Points
Locations where the Root's influence breaks through:
- **[[The Greenward]]** — Aldenmire's north common. Roots in perfect geometric spirals. Black grass. A faceless figure at midnight. The earth is wrong here. **DM-only:** this is also where the new vessel, [[Princess Kallista]], is stirring closest to the surface.

With two of three destroyed, the seal is under increasing strain — by design.

## The Old Chord Shrine
[[The Old Chord]] shrine was built to hold the seal's focal point — not to worship anything, but to **contain**. The central stone has three triangular indentations sized for the three instruments. It predates Aldenmire; the town accreted around it without knowing why people kept settling on this spot. **DM-only:** the collapsed section holds records of the original seal-keepers and, eventually, the first hard evidence of the Hollow Crown.

## The Gilding (the Crown's signature)
The Crown's power, bleeding out, looks like **golden light that hardens into amber/resin** — it preserves and it binds. This is the visual tell that lets you foreshadow the true enemy long before naming it:
- [[Corvin Fels|Corvin]]'s "Gilded Binding" in [[Session 02 — Standard Procedure]] — the party's first contact with it.
- [[Kallista's Brooch]] — a mushroom in resin; amber is the Crown's medium of preservation.
- [[Amoris Scilia|Amoris]]'s brand aches/flares near gilded magic — same source.

## The Faceless Figures
Branded changelings are drawn to resonance points; their shapeshifting destabilises near the seal, blurring their outlines. The figure [[Maret]] saw was not a monster — a frightened person with a rune brand like [[Amoris Scilia|Amoris]]'s. **DM-only:** these brands are all transfer-spillover — failed or partial Crown-transfers leave the same mark. Amoris is not the only one who was standing too close to a ritual.

## DM Notes
- **Act 1:** leave the seal unexplained. Let the Greenward be unsettling, the Old Chord hum, Amoris's brand ache, the gold appear without context.
- **Act 2:** the Old Chord becomes a major lore location; the keystone concept enters play; seed the [[Oswin Dray|Oswin]] red herring.
- **Act 3:** the bearer is revealed (see [[The Hollow Crown]]). The destruction of the instruments resolves into the engineered-transfer reveal.
- Open design questions live in [[The Hollow Crown]].
