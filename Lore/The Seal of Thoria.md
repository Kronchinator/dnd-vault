# The Seal of Thoria

## Overview
Something is imprisoned beneath [[Thoria]]. The seal that holds it is old — older than [[Aldenmire]], older than any current religious tradition in the region. Its influence bleeds through resonance points where the earth is thin.

The party does not know about this yet. Do not explain it in Act 1.

## The Resonance Points
Locations where the imprisoned entity's influence breaks through:
- **[[The Greenward]]** — Aldenmire's north common. Roots growing in perfect geometric spirals. Black grass. A faceless figure seen at midnight. The earth is wrong here.
- *(Others to be established in later acts)*

## The Components
The seal requires three instruments to be maintained. These are the [[Ravendeer Clan]]'s sacred instruments — though the Ravendeers themselves may not have understood their full significance:

| Component | Instrument | Current State |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| Voice | [[Lyre of Veilsong]] | Intact — held by Aeritch |
| Calm | [[Flute of Stillwater]] | Destroyed — husk held by Aeritch |
| Earth | [[Drum of Rootsong]] | Destroyed — fragments held by Aeritch |

With two of the three destroyed, the seal is under increasing strain.

## The Old Chord Shrine
[[The Old Chord]] shrine in Aldenmire was built to hold the seal's focal point — not to worship anything, but to contain it. The central stone has three triangular indentations sized for the three instruments. The shrine predates Aldenmire itself; the town was built around it without knowing why people kept settling there.

## The Faceless Figures
Branded changelings are drawn to resonance points — their shapeshifting destabilises near the seal's influence, making their outlines blur and shift. The figure [[Maret]] saw was not a monster; it was a frightened person with a rune brand like [[Amoris Scilia|Amoris]]'s.

The rune brand itself may be connected to the seal — this has not yet been established.

## DM Notes
- Act 1: leave the seal unexplained. Let the Greenward be unsettling. Let the Old Chord hum. Let Amoris's brand ache.
- Act 2: the Old Chord shrine becomes a major lore location. Records of the original seal-keepers exist in the collapsed section if excavated.
- The destruction of two of the three instruments is the central crisis driving the seal's weakening
- Whether destroyed instruments can be restored, replaced, or substituted is a design question for Acts 2–3
