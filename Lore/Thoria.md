# Thoria

## Overview
The world is old. Older than the kingdoms that carved it up, older than the roads that cross it, older than the names people gave the rivers and the mountains. Most people who live in it don't think about that. They think about the harvest, the weather, the price of grain at market.

## Geography (Known)
- **[[Aldenmire]]** — crossroads town where the Greyvein river meets the north merchant road; Act 1 location
- **[[Ravencroft]]** — kingdom near Aldenmire's border; Amoris's home
- **[[Meridh]]** — modest port town; where Aeritch spent years after her family's death
- The Greyvein river — runs through Aldenmire; the Albertich tax post sits on its bridge
- The north merchant road — major trade route passing through Aldenmire

## Factions (Known)
- **[[Albertich Clan]]** — ruthless mercantile family, current regional antagonist
- **[[Ravendeer Clan]]** — destroyed; Aeritch's family; once commanded regional trade

## Undercurrents
Something old is underneath Thoria. The [[Seal of Thoria]] holds something imprisoned — and it is not holding as well as it once did. The evidence is in the earth: [[The Greenward]] in Aldenmire, black-rooted and geometrically wrong, sits above one of the seal's resonance points.

The people of Thoria don't know about the seal. They think about the harvest.

## DM Notes
- The world's age is a theme — institutions, families, grudges, and ancient things all predate the current moment
- The campaign operates at the ground level (merchant conflicts, local oppression) before scaling to the cosmic level (the seal, the imprisoned god)
- Reveal Thoria's deeper history slowly through locations like [[The Old Chord]] shrine and through Ressi's Religion checks
