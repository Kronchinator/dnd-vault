# Brek

## Description
Large, calm, and built like someone who has been in a lot of fights and stopped being surprised by them. He wears studded leather, keeps a scimitar on his hip and a heavy crossbow across his back. His face is forgettable on purpose — the kind of face that has learned not to announce itself.

He doesn't posture. He doesn't threaten loudly. He simply recalculates when things don't go to plan, and keeps moving.

## Personality
- Even-tempered to the point of being unsettling — anger would mean caring
- Professional; this is a job, not a vendetta (though the party has made it personal)
- Knows when a fight isn't worth finishing and retreats without shame
- Respects competence in opponents, which makes him more dangerous, not less

## Goals
- Locate and detain Aeritch for delivery to the Ashwing
- Maintain Albertich enforcement operations in the Aldenmire region
- Report the party's capabilities accurately to his superiors

## Combat Stats
- AC 14 (studded leather), HP 22, Speed 30 ft
- Initiative: +2
- **Scimitar:** +4 to hit, 1d6+2 slashing
- **Heavy Crossbow:** +3 to hit, 1d10+1 piercing, range 100/400
- **Parry (Reaction):** When hit by a melee attack, +2 AC against that attack. Once per round.
- **Morale:** Retreats at 11 HP (half). Will not die for this assignment.

## Secrets
- Brek knows more about why Aeritch is being sought than he's been told officially — he's overheard enough to know it connects to the Ashwing's employer, not just the Albertich clan's trade interests
- He has a genuine respect for Aeritch's survival instincts; in a different world he might say so
- He files complete reports — the party's abilities, their faces, their rough class breakdown — all of this goes up the chain after Session 1

## Relationship to Players
The party drove him out of the Tillerman's Rest. He left without losing his composure. He will not underestimate them again.

**His exit line, Session 1:** *"You're all going to want to remember this night. Corvin's going to hear about this. Sleep well."*

He meant it as documentation, not a threat. The distinction is worse.
