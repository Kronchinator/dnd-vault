# Corvin Fels

## Description
Lean, fastidiously clean-shaved, always in a well-cut coat that costs more than the people he's collecting from can afford. He moves like a man who has never been in a hurry because he has never needed to be. His hands are ink-stained from the ledger; his nails are always clean.

He has a very pleasant smile. He uses it constantly. It does not reach his eyes.

## Personality
- Speaks softly and precisely; never raises his voice
- Documents everything — the ledger is his weapon of choice
- Not personally cruel; he simply treats human suffering as a line item
- Deeply competent and takes pride in it
- Dislikes disorder; the party fighting back in Session 1 has genuinely offended his sense of process

## Goals
- Maintain Albertich revenue streams in Aldenmire at or above target
- Identify and neutralise threats to Albertich operations (the party is now a threat)
- Advance within the Albertich hierarchy — Aldenmire is a stepping stone

## Secrets
- The ledger in the back room contains Aeritch's name with a note: *"High priority. Do not approach directly. Notify Brek."* He did not write this — it arrived from above him.
- Corvin has private doubts about [[The Ashwing]]'s methods but would never say so
- He has identified the Albertich informant in Aldenmire and uses them regularly — he considers this good asset management

## How to Play Corvin
Corvin is most frightening when he is polite. He should never threaten directly — he documents, he notes, he smiles. The violence is always implied, always delegated. When the party has made an enemy of him, he writes their names in the ledger. That is the moment to feel afraid.

**Dialogue examples:**
- *"I hope you're enjoying Aldenmire. Lovely town."*
- *"I'll note your interest in Mister Dray's accounts."*
- *"That's not what the arrangement says."*
- *"Standard procedure."*

## Relationship to Players
Following Session 1, Corvin knows the party exists, has their rough descriptions, and has sent a report to Brek. He will not come for them directly. He will make Aldenmire uncomfortable for them in small, documented ways.
