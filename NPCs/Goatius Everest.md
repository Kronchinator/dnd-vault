# Goatius Everest

## Player-Facing Status

The party has not formally met Goatius.

He has been nearby the entire time: across a street, on a roofline, behind a cart, among other animals, or just far enough away that nobody thinks to count him as important. If noticed too early, he should appear to be an unusually stubborn goat rather than the linchpin of Melogg's existence.

The rune on the underside of [[Goatius's Stone]] is connected to him.

---

## Description

Goatius appears, at first glance, to be a goat.

This is technically true in the same way a lit torch is technically a stick.

He is compact, hard-headed, and much harder to kill than a goat should be. His eyes are too aware. He watches conversations like he is waiting for the stupidest person in the room to reveal themselves. His horns are scuffed, his coat carries faint old burn marks, and in certain light there may be a trace of pentagram-red around his hooves.

He is usually near Melogg but rarely beside him openly. Goatius knows the tether range and treats the 100-metre limit like a leash, a workplace safety regulation, and a personal insult all at once.

---

## Personality

- Blunt, stubborn, and almost impossible to intimidate.
- Motivated by simple appetites, survival, and getting home.
- Has no patience for cultists, bureaucracy, or anyone who overcomplicates a deal.
- Cares about Melogg, though he may express this by headbutting problems until they become simpler.
- Does not think of himself as noble. Unfortunately, he sometimes is.

---

## Goals

- Keep Melogg within the 100-metre tether range.
- Get back to **[[Mount Veyska]]**.
- Find the finest goats Mount Veyska has to offer. This is not subtle. Neither is Goatius.
- Help [[Matthew]] find his boss, because that was the bargain.
- Survive long enough to make all of the above everyone else's problem.

---

## True Backstory — DM Only

Goatius was once an ordinary goat from **[[Mount Veyska]]**.

A group of cultists captured him for use in a ritual sacrifice. Their goal was to summon the all-powerful demon **[[Matthew]]**. Against the better interests of everyone present, the ritual worked.

Matthew arrived successfully and immediately killed all the cultists — not out of grand demonic malice, but because he was frustrated, overworked, and unable to find his boss to submit his two weeks' notice. The cultists had expected an apocalypse. What they got was middle management collapse with horns.

After the cultists were dead, Matthew noticed Goatius lying dead in the summoning pentagram. Rather than waste a perfectly usable goat-shaped opportunity, Matthew resurrected him.

Matthew then formed a deal with Goatius:

- Goatius would help Matthew find his boss.
- Matthew would help Goatius return to Mount Veyska.
- Matthew would remain close enough to ensure the bargain was upheld.

Goatius accepted because he wanted to go home. Specifically, he wanted to return to Mount Veyska and resume the highly sophisticated mountain-goat social life he had been so rudely removed from.

---

## After the Deal — DM Only

Matthew travelled with Goatius after the bargain. To protect the arrangement, he helped create **[[Melogg]]**: a golem companion, protector, and failsafe.

Matthew sealed a fragment of himself inside Melogg. The fragment functions like a demonic emergency phone — not full possession, not constant control, but a way for Matthew to remain connected if Melogg or Goatius falls into serious danger.

This means Melogg is not only Goatius's companion. He is also Matthew's contingency plan.

Goatius knows more than Melogg does, but he may not understand every consequence. He knows there was a bargain. He knows Matthew made Melogg. He knows staying near Melogg matters. Whether he understands the ethics of manufacturing a person around a demonic failsafe is, frankly, optimistic.

---

## Secrets

- **The Tether** — Melogg is mystically bound to Goatius at a range of **100 metres**. If Melogg moves beyond this distance, Melogg cannot move or speak. The party does not know this.
- **The Stone** — Goatius gave Melogg [[Goatius's Stone]] deliberately; it is a tracking anchor that keeps the tether stable.
- **Proximity Tell** — The warmth of the stone increases when Goatius is within 30 feet.
- **Matthew's Fragment** — A fragment of [[Matthew]] is sealed inside Melogg as a failsafe/communication anchor.
- **Goatius's Death** — If Goatius dies again, the tether may snap, lock Melogg in place, awaken Matthew's fragment, or call Matthew directly.
- **Melogg's Memories** — Goatius may know that Melogg's Zotseudan backstory is false, altered, or incomplete.

---

## Relationship to Melogg

Goatius and Melogg are genuinely companions, even if the origin is deeply suspect.

The fake/altered backstory says Goatius saved Melogg from hunters and became his travelling partner. This may have been implanted, partially true, or built from real events after Melogg's creation. Either way, their bond has become real through use. A forged chain can still hold.

Goatius should not be played as secretly evil. He is selfish, crude, and hiding things, but he is not trying to destroy Melogg. He wants to get home. He wants the bargain fulfilled. He does not want Melogg dead.

That still leaves plenty of room for him to be a tremendous problem.

## Reveal Guidance

Early signs:

- Melogg's stone warms when no obvious source is nearby.
- A goat is seen at the edge of scenes and vanishes before anyone can approach.
- Animals react oddly to a direction the party cannot explain.
- Melogg feels a sudden stillness when he unknowingly nears the tether limit.

Mid reveal:

- The party almost catches Goatius observing them.
- Someone identifies the rune on [[Goatius's Stone]] as demonic-adjacent but not quite infernal script.
- Melogg briefly hears Matthew's voice through the stone or inside his own body during danger.

Full reveal:

- Goatius speaks or otherwise reveals intelligence.
- The tether is exposed by distance, danger, or Matthew's intervention.
- Melogg learns that the goat from his remembered backstory is not merely a funny companion, but the anchor around which his entire existence was arranged.
