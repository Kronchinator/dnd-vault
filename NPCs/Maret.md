# Maret

## Description
Old enough that her age is hard to guess — somewhere between sixty and eighty. Her hair is white and pinned back practically. She moves slowly but precisely, without waste. Her house is dim and very neat, and smells like dried herbs and old paper.

She has a chair facing the window that looks out at the Greenward. There is a worn patch on the floorboards there.

## Personality
- Speaks plainly; does not dramatise what she has seen because she is still not sure she believes it herself
- Will only talk inside, with the door closed
- Observant in a way that suggests a lifetime of paying attention to things others ignore
- Not frightened — or rather, she has had three weeks to process being frightened and has arrived at grim curiosity

## Goals
- Understand what she saw on the Greenward before it gets worse
- Keep the children in Aldenmire away from whatever is happening
- Find someone who will take her seriously — the party is the first

## Secrets
- She keeps a journal of everything she has observed at the Greenward; it goes back further than three weeks — she has been watching something wrong with the ground for nearly a year, but the faceless figure was new
- She found something at the spiral centre two weeks ago: a small piece of dark root twisted into a perfect circle, like a ring. She kept it. She hasn't shown anyone.
- Her late husband was a druid; she learned enough from him to know that what's happening at the Greenward is not natural decay

## The Sketch
Maret drew the faceless figure from memory on the back of an old letter. The figure is blurred at the edges in her drawing — she says that's how it looked, not an artistic choice. Its outline was indistinct, like it was *between* shapes.

*(DM: the figure was a branded changeling drawn to the Greenward's resonance point. The blurred outline is the changeling's shapeshifting destabilised by proximity to the seal.)*

## Relationship to Players
Maret will speak to the party if they come to her. She asks nothing in return except that they take what she tells them seriously and do something about it.

**What she shares freely:**
- The account of the faceless figure, including the sketch
- The fact that the grass has been wrong for months, not just recently
- That her late husband would have called this "deep ground sickness"

**What she needs to be pressed for:**
- The root-circle she found at the spiral centre
- Her year-long journal of observations
