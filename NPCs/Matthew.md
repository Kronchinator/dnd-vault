# Matthew

## Summary

Matthew is an extremely powerful demon who was summoned by cultists attempting to perform a grand sacrifice using [[Goatius Everest]]. The ritual succeeded. The cultists did not enjoy the result.

Matthew killed them almost immediately because he was furious, distracted, and trying to find his boss to submit his two weeks' notice. This is ridiculous. It is also potentially campaign-important, which is usually how these things become dangerous.

---

## Personality

- Overworked, irritated, and far too powerful for his current emotional regulation.
- Treats cosmic horror like an administrative inconvenience.
- Capable of sudden, devastating violence when annoyed.
- Pragmatic enough to resurrect a goat because the situation looked useful.
- May be more bound by deals and procedure than he wants to admit.

---

## Connection to Goatius

After killing the cultists, Matthew found Goatius dead in the summoning pentagram. Rather than waste the opportunity, he resurrected Goatius and made a bargain with him:

- Goatius helps Matthew find his boss.
- Matthew helps Goatius return to [[Mount Veyska]].
- Matthew ensures the bargain remains enforceable.

Matthew then travelled with Goatius for some time to make sure the deal did not fall apart.

---

## Connection to Melogg

Matthew helped create [[Melogg]] after the bargain with Goatius.

He sealed a fragment of himself inside Melogg, functioning like a demonic emergency phone. If Melogg or Goatius is seriously harmed, killed, or pushed into a magically significant state, Matthew may be able to sense it, speak through it, or intervene.

This fragment is not necessarily possession. It is better treated as a failsafe, anchor, and communication line — unless the story later needs it to become worse. Keep that door unlocked, but do not walk through it too early.

## Open Questions

- Who is Matthew's boss?
- Why does Matthew want to resign?
- What happens if Matthew's boss finds him first?
- Did Matthew create Melogg from nothing, alter a pre-existing golem, or bind a soul into stone?
- How much of Melogg's fake backstory did Matthew design deliberately?

---

## DM Tone Guidance

Matthew should be funny because his priorities are absurd, not because he is harmless.

He is still a demon powerful enough to slaughter a cult and casually alter life and death. The comedy is the wrapper. The blade is inside.
