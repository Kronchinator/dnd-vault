# Pell

## Description
About fourteen, with a dockworker's father and too much time between shifts. Wiry, quick-eyed, and permanently underdressed for the weather. He knows every alley, shortcut, and loose fence board in Aldenmire. He is in places he is not supposed to be with remarkable consistency.

## Personality
- Curious about everything; the party is the most interesting thing that's happened in months
- Negotiates fairly but always negotiates — information has a price, even if it's just a meal
- Loyal once loyalty is established; will run errands, carry messages, keep watch
- Pretends to know more than he does until he actually does know more than he does

## Goals
- Get enough coin together to leave Aldenmire eventually
- Not get beaten up by Albertich enforcers (has been once already; would prefer not to repeat it)
- Find out what's happening at the Greenward because it scares him and he can't stop thinking about it

## Location
Currently operates out of the ruins of the Merchant's Guild — the old guild building is a shell, but Pell uses what's left of it. Oswin mentioned this to the party in Session 1.

## What Pell Knows
- Which guards rotate at what time at the Weighing House
- That Corvin Fels leaves by the east door at dusk, every day
- The name of the merchant who got beaten on the north road (and three others who got the same treatment)
- That the Albertich collectors have a second location — a barn outside town where they hold goods before moving them
- He went to the Greenward once on a dare and doesn't talk about what he saw there

## Secrets
- What he saw at the Greenward: a patch of black grass in the shape of a handprint, far too large to be human. He drew it on the inside of his boot.
- He overheard two Albertich enforcers mention "the woman with the lyre" three days before the party arrived — someone told them she was coming

## Relationship to Players
Pell will approach the party on his own by Session 2 if they seem interesting — he's been watching them since they arrived. He can be recruited as a local contact and errand runner.

**Price for information:** Usually 2-5 copper per piece, or a hot meal, or something interesting in return.
