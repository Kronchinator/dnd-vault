## Backstory

### Player-Facing Backstory — What Melogg Believes

Melogg comes from the kingdom of **[[Zotseudan]]**, a real land of fertile fields and steady prosperity. No one in Zotseudan lived in poverty, yet no one possessed excessive wealth either. Its people lived simple, comfortable lives, content with what they had and suspicious of anyone who needed too much more.

As an infant, Melogg was abandoned on the doorstep of an orphanage called **[[The House of Saint Edith]]**. He never knew his real parents, but he grew up surrounded by children he came to see as brothers and sisters. The orphanage was not rich, but it was warm enough. For a time, that was enough.

One afternoon, Melogg mocked the ragged clothes of a wandering witch, laughing at her tattered appearance without understanding who she truly was. The witch, deeply insulted, cursed him on the spot. Before his eyes, his body twisted into stone, moss, and earth, transforming him into a living golem.

Terrified by what he had become, Melogg fled back to The House of Saint Edith hoping for comfort and reassurance. Instead, the sight of him sent the children and caretakers into panic. Believing him to be a monster, they drove him away from the orphanage and banished him from Zotseudan entirely.

Alone and frightened, Melogg wandered deep into the forests beyond the kingdom. Over the years, he discovered that his new body no longer hungered, thirsted, or tired. He did not need to breathe. Time passed differently for him now. Seasons came and went while moss crept across his stone body and flowers bloomed from the cracks in his shoulders.

For a long time, Melogg lived in complete isolation.

Everything changed when he met a goat.

The two first encountered each other when the goat attempted to eat a flower growing from Melogg's moss-covered back while he was trying to sleep. Their relationship began poorly, with Melogg more annoyed than amused, but the stubborn little creature refused to leave him alone.

Eventually, the goat proved itself more than a nuisance.

One day, a group of explorers mistook Melogg for a dangerous monster they had been hunting and attempted to kill him for the bounty. Before they could finish the job, the goat intervened, distracting the hunters long enough for Melogg to escape. From that moment onward, the two became inseparable companions.

Together, they tracked down the real monster, claimed its bounty, and discovered they made an unexpectedly effective team. From then on, Melogg and the goat travelled across the wilderness hunting creatures, collecting rewards, and wandering wherever the roads carried them.

Though they earned plenty of money over the years, they rarely spent any of it. Melogg had already lost his place among people long ago, and neither he nor the goat desired wealth or comfort. The forests, mountains, and open skies were enough for them.

### True Backstory — DM Only

The story above is the version Melogg believes, or at least the version that has been made stable enough for him to live inside.

The deeper truth is tied to **[[Goatius Everest]]**, **[[Matthew]]**, and the 100-metre tether. Melogg was not simply a cursed orphan from Zotseudan. He was created with demonic assistance after Matthew made a bargain with Goatius.

Goatius was originally an ordinary goat from **[[Mount Veyska]]**. A cult attempted to sacrifice him in a ritual meant to summon the all-powerful demon Matthew. The ritual succeeded, but Matthew immediately killed the cultists because he was preoccupied, furious, and apparently unable to locate his boss to submit his two weeks' notice. A demon with workplace grievances. Naturally, everyone died.

Matthew then saw Goatius dead in the summoning pentagram and decided the situation might still be useful. He brought Goatius back to life and formed a deal with him: Goatius would help Matthew find his boss, and Matthew would eventually help Goatius return home to Mount Veyska.

Goatius's own goal is extremely direct: he wants to get back to his mountain and find the finest goats Mount Veyska has to offer. This should remain funny on the surface, but useful underneath: Goatius is crude, stubborn, and ridiculous, yet his desire for home is real.

After the deal, Matthew stayed close to Goatius to make sure the bargain was upheld. As part of that arrangement, Matthew helped create Melogg: a golem companion, protector, and insurance policy. Matthew sealed a fragment of himself inside Melogg, functioning almost like a demonic emergency phone. If Melogg or Goatius falls into serious harm — possibly even death — Matthew has a way to notice, intervene, or reclaim the situation.

This makes Melogg both person and contingency plan. He may have genuine memories, feelings, and attachments, but his origin has been shaped by a bargain he does not understand.

### The Tether — DM Only

- Melogg is mystically tethered to **[[Goatius Everest]]** at a range of **100 metres**.
- If Melogg moves beyond this range, he **cannot move or speak**.
- Goatius is always nearby, even when the party has not noticed him.
- [[Goatius's Stone]] helps stabilize the tether and acts as a tracking anchor.
- The fragment of [[Matthew]] inside Melogg is separate from the stone, but the two effects may resonate.
- If Melogg dies or Goatius is seriously harmed, Matthew may become aware through the fragment.

### DM Tone Guidance

This backstory should run on two engines at once:

1. **Absurd comedy:** a sacrificed goat, a demon trying to quit his job, and a mountain goat with deeply unserious romantic priorities.
2. **Quiet horror:** Melogg may be a manufactured person whose identity was built around a false or altered history.

Do not flatten either side. The joke works better because the implications are horrible. The horror works better because everyone involved is an idiot.

## DM Notes

### ⚠️ SECRET — PARTY DOES NOT KNOW

- **Tether to Goatius Everest** — Melogg is mystically tethered to **[[Goatius Everest]]**. Range: 100 metres. If Melogg moves beyond this range, he **cannot move or speak**. Goatius is always present near Melogg — the party does not know this and should not be told yet.
- **Matthew's Fragment** — A fragment of **[[Matthew]]** is sealed inside Melogg as a demonic failsafe/communication anchor. It may trigger if Melogg or Goatius suffers serious harm.
- **False/Altered History** — Melogg's Zotseudan backstory may be partially true, fully fabricated, or implanted as a stabilizing identity. Keep this flexible until the campaign needs a harder answer.

### Open Questions
- Is Zotseudan fully real, partially real, or a memory-structure used to stabilize Melogg?
- Was The House of Saint Edith real, or a false memory grafted onto Melogg?
- Did Matthew create Melogg from scratch, alter an existing golem, or bind a real soul into stone?
- What exactly does Matthew's boss want, and why is Matthew so desperate to resign?
- How does Melogg's Large size affect the party? (Squeezing through doors, seating at taverns, etc.)
