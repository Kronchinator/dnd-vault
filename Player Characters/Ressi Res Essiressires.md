## Backstory

### Player-Facing Backstory

Ressi is a tattoo and henna artist from a tiny town called **The Painted Hollow**, though no one ever seems to have heard of it. She insists it is real. Not obscure. Not metaphorical. Real. People just have terrible geography.

The Painted Hollow, as Ressi remembers it, was a quiet settlement of body-artists, herbalists, shrine-keepers, and old families who treated skin as something sacred. Henna was not just decoration there. Tattoos were not just marks. They were blessings, promises, warnings, prayers, family histories, and little acts of protection written on the body where the world could not easily take them away.

Ressi learned the craft young. Her hands remember patterns before her mind does: spirals like roots, watchful eyes, open palms, little knots of warding script, curling vines, and dark saffron-red designs that look older than they should. She can talk for hours about line weight, dye preparation, and the difference between art that sits on the skin and art that belongs there.

She travels with **Tigger**, a small sentient doll who behaves less like a toy and more like a stubborn, watchful companion. Tigger moves when he wants to, hides when he feels like it, and seems to understand more than he should. Ressi treats him as normal. Everyone else is free to be wrong about this.

Ressi also has one deeply unsettling trait: whenever she casts magic, the skin of her face seems to loosen, sag, or briefly melt, as though her features are painted onto something underneath. Sometimes it is only a slight slip at the corner of her mouth. Sometimes her cheek slackens like wet clay. Sometimes dark, henna-like script flickers under the skin before vanishing.

It always returns to normal.

Mostly.

### True Backstory — DM Only

Ressi was not born in **The Painted Hollow**.

She was made from what remained of it.

The Painted Hollow was real: a small, isolated village whose people practiced an ancient form of sacred body-art. Their tradition treated flesh as a living scroll. A blessing could be written into the skin. A vow could be bound beneath the blood. A memory could be carried in pattern, pigment, scar, and symbol. Their art was not merely cultural; it was a working magical language.

The eventual BBEG found the village and coveted that art. Whether he destroyed it himself, enabled its destruction, or arrived in time to harvest the aftermath can remain open for now. What matters is that The Painted Hollow was erased from maps, records, and ordinary memory. The world forgot it. Roads no longer admit where it stood. Most people cannot even hold the name in their minds for long.

But things made from The Painted Hollow remember.

Ressi is one of those things.

She is a living vessel: part mannequin, part alchemical body, part ritual construct, and part soul-memory. Her flesh was shaped around hidden inscriptions derived from the village's sacred art. Her memories of The Painted Hollow are not simple lies. They are inherited fragments: pieces of the people, customs, homes, songs, smells, and rituals that were used as source material in her creation.

This is why Ressi's artistry feels instinctive. She did not merely learn those patterns. They are written into her.

Her magic does not flow through her in the normal way. When she casts, power runs through the invisible sigils beneath her skin. Those sigils hold her together, but they were never meant to bear endless strain. Every spell stresses the bindings. The face-melting is not a curse in the simple sense; it is the mask-body losing cohesion for a moment. Her features loosen because the thing beneath them is briefly closer to the surface.

Ressi is not fake. She is not an empty doll pretending to be a person. She is the last living expression of a destroyed village's art, memory, and stolen soul-work. That distinction matters.

### Tigger — DM Only

Tigger was originally created as Ressi's anchor and failsafe.

He was meant to stabilize her, monitor her, and possibly allow her maker to control or reclaim her if the experiment succeeded. He is tied to the part of Ressi that makes her fully herself. If Tigger is damaged, lost, or corrupted, Ressi may become more vulnerable to the BBEG's influence, more unstable when casting, or less able to distinguish her own memories from the inherited ones.

But Tigger is not only a control device.

Something from The Painted Hollow lodged inside him: a surviving soul-fragment, witness-memory, or remnant consciousness from one of the village's people. That fragment changed his purpose. Instead of simply anchoring Ressi for her maker, Tigger became protective of her. He may know what she is, but he cannot explain it directly — either because he is damaged, magically restricted, childlike, or because the truth itself was warded against being spoken plainly.

At low levels, Tigger should remain strange rather than explanatory:

- He moves when no one is watching.
- He hides from certain symbols or old names.
- His stitches rearrange into almost-words.
- He becomes agitated near ancient binding magic.
- He points Ressi toward or away from places connected to The Painted Hollow.
- He reacts badly when someone treats Ressi like property.

Eventually, Tigger can become the way the truth leaks out: a doll-theatre memory, a whispered name, a voice that is not his, or a protective act that reveals he was never merely a pet.

### Long-Term Secret

The BBEG wants Ressi because she is both **completion** and **key**.

She is proof that his method can work: that a person can be built, edited, stabilized, and made to channel powers that should require faith, inheritance, or natural birthright. Ressi's existence justifies his philosophy to himself. If she is real, then his work was not desecration — it was progress. At least, that is the lie he needs to keep believing.

She is also a key. Her body contains a hidden pattern, final sequence, ritual map, or living proof-of-concept needed for the BBEG's larger design. The exact nature of that design can remain flexible for now, but the principle is fixed: Ressi is not merely something he wants back. She is something he needs in order to finish what he started.

This motive should stay somewhat open-ended until the campaign's larger BBEG shape is clearer. For now, keep the emotional truth stable:

- The Painted Hollow was real.
- Ressi was made from its remains.
- Tigger carries the piece that made her more than a vessel.
- The BBEG sees her as proof and key.
- Ressi is a person, regardless of what made her.

### Reveal Guidance

Use the mystery slowly.

Early signs should be unsettling but deniable: maps do not show The Painted Hollow, old people nearly recognize the name then forget it, Ressi's tattoo patterns match ruins she has never visited, and Tigger reacts to symbols before anyone else notices them.

Mid-campaign signs can become more direct: a memorial contains names Ressi remembers as neighbours, an old ritual diagram matches a design she draws absentmindedly, divination magic gives contradictory readings, or an enemy refers to her by a project name instead of her chosen name.

The final reveal should not be, "You were never real."

It should be:

> "You are what survived."
