# Session 02 — Standard Procedure

## DM Cheat Sheet — One Page Version

**Purpose:** make the party hate [[Corvin Fels|Corvin]], reveal that the Alberich problem is backed by something much larger, capture the party, then give them an immediate escape problem to solve.

**Core shape:**
1. Morning at [[The Tillerman's Rest]]. Corvin arrives to collect.
2. Real opening fight vs. Alberich enforcers.
3. Corvin uses impossible gold/amber binding magic and the fight becomes unwinnable.
4. [[Barkimedes]] disrupts Corvin for one doomed player choice.
5. Corvin kills Barkimedes to humiliate [[Oswin Dray|Oswin]] and the party.
6. [[Brek]] arrives, reins Corvin in, disarms the party, and moves them to [[The Weighing House]].
7. In the cell, the party gets agency back fast. [[Pell]] becomes the escape vector.
8. End with the breakout in motion or the first major choice after escape.

**Safety check before play:** this session contains helplessness and animal cruelty. Tell the table first: *"This campaign goes dark sometimes, including moments where your characters cannot save someone. If anything is too much, say pause and we step back."* If the table reacts badly, Barkimedes is maimed/stunned/taken instead of killed. No need to be precious about cruelty; the villain can function without traumatizing the humans at the table. Annoying, but true.

## Scene Flow and Timing

### Beat 0 — Cold Open: Morning After

**Read-aloud:**
> The fire in the Tillerman's Rest has burned down to coals. Grey light comes through the shutters the colour of dishwater. Oswin is up — he's been up — moving behind the bar with the careful quiet of a man trying not to wake the trouble he knows is coming. Barkimedes is under the bar, chin on his paws, ears tracking the door. Then boots. More than last night. Not trying to be quiet.

**DM aim:** remind them Corvin gave Oswin until morning. It is morning. Do not let the party wander away before consequence arrives unless they made a brilliant preventative plan last session.

### Beat 1 — Corvin Collects

**Read-aloud:**
> The door opens and the cold comes in with it. Four grey-coated enforcers file in and spread along the wall like they've done it a hundred times. Last comes Corvin Fels — coat brushed, nails clean, ledger under one arm. He sets the ledger on the bar, opens it, and smooths the page flat.  
> "Mister Dray. The sum, plus the late assessment. And there's the separate matter of the disturbance on these premises last night. Property damage. Assault on Alberich personnel." He finally turns to the party. "I did say I'd note your interest."

**If the party negotiates:** Corvin accepts nothing emotionally. He adds fees. If they offer coin, he documents it and still demands surrender for the assault. If they refuse to escalate, an enforcer lays hands on Oswin or starts taking property as collateral.

### Beat 2 — The Fight They Can Win

Run straight for 2–3 rounds. Let them drop one or two enforcers. The win must feel real before it is taken away.

**Enemy lineup:** Corvin + 4 Alberich Enforcers. Brek is not present yet.

**Tactics:**
- Enforcers fight badly once the party hits back hard.
- Corvin stays behind the line with crossbow/ledger, issuing orders.
- Corvin only uses the Gilding once the party looks like they are winning or he is personally threatened.

### Beat 3 — The Gilding

**Read-aloud:**
> Corvin sighs, the way a man sighs at a stain. He closes the ledger. He pulls off one glove, finger by finger, and lays his bare hand flat on the bar.  
> The wood gilds. Gold runs out from under his palm in fine veins, like cracks filling with molten light, and the air goes thick and sweet — resin, sap, something floral and wrong. The gold leaves the bar. It finds you. It crawls up boots and wrists and throats and hardens, clear and amber and shot through with gold, until every one of you is held like an insect set in stone.

**Mechanical ruling:** this is a scripted capture set-piece, not a fair spell. If players ask to roll, allow STR save / spellcasting checks at DC 18–20; failure tightens the amber. Success gives a tiny narrative advantage, not escape.

**Seeds to fire silently:**
- [[Amoris Scilia|Amoris]]: the rune on her hand flares hot and almost pulls toward the gold.
- [[Ressi Res Essiressires|Ressi]]: her face slips; henna-script flickers under the skin. Let one PC glimpse it.
- Sensory clue: resin / preserved flower smell. Use same scent later for [[The Old Chord]] and [[Kallista's Brooch]].
- Corvin shows reverence/fear for the power. It is borrowed from the [[The Hollow Crown|Gilded King]] / Architect. Do not explain.

### Beat 4 — Barkimedes' Doomed Opening

**Read-aloud:**
> You hear him before you see him — a low building sound from under the bar that isn't a growl so much as a decision. Then Barkimedes comes over the bar in one ugly heave, all head and shoulders and no plan, and goes straight for the man making the bad smell.  
> Corvin's concentration stumbles. The gold flickers — for half a heartbeat the amber on all of you goes soft as warm honey.

**Give exactly one fast party choice:** ask, *"For one second, one of you can move a hand, say a word, or reach for something. Who takes it, and what do they do?"*

**Ruling:** their choice lands emotionally and fails mechanically. A word reaches Oswin. A thrown object disrupts an enforcer's stance. A spell sparks and dies. Then the amber locks harder.

### Beat 5 — Oswin Begs

**Read-aloud:**
> The kitchen door bangs. Oswin is there, and the steadiness the party has only ever seen on him is gone. He takes in the gold, the patrons set in it like flies, his dog pinned and bleeding, and something in him folds. He comes around the bar and gets down on his knees. On the floor of his own inn.  
> "Please. Let the patrons go. They're good people. They didn't — it was my fault, all of it. I'll get the money. I'll get it, I swear to every god, just — let them go. Let my dog up. Please."

Let this sit. Do not rush it.

### Beat 6 — Corvin's Cruelty

**Read-aloud:**
> Corvin looks at the kneeling man for a long moment. And then he laughs — delighted, almost surprised, like Oswin has done something genuinely funny.  
> "Gods, you people. You grovel. It's extraordinary." He shakes his head, still smiling. "Very well then. Since you asked so nicely."  
> He turns to his men. "Kill the dog."

If the enforcer hesitates, Corvin does it himself. Keep the description spare. The horror is that Corvin is physically weak, bad at it, and continues calmly anyway. No gore needed.

### Beat 7 — Brek Arrives and Captures Them Properly

**Read-aloud:**
> The door opens one more time. Brek steps in, takes in the scene in a single sweep, and his face does something tired. "Fels." Flat. "What is this."  
> Corvin opens his mouth. Brek raises a hand. "Pack it up. The elf's flagged — that means she goes nowhere and you touch nothing until the Ashwing's word comes down. You know that." He looks at the gilded party, then the dog, then back at Corvin. "Get them off the floor and put them somewhere they'll keep."

**Capture:** the gold drops; crossbows are up; the party is disarmed, bound, and walked to the Weighing House. Aeritch is treated as separate/high-value. Oswin is left kneeling.

## Places — Fast Descriptions

### [[The Tillerman's Rest]]
Only inn in Aldenmire. Wide common room, flagstone floor, heavy oak tables, long bar across the back, fire always burning low. Smells of woodsmoke, tallow, and cheap stew. It should feel like the party's one safe place — which is why Corvin violating it works.

**Combat features:**
- Bar: half cover (+2 AC).
- Tables: can be flipped for half cover as bonus action.
- Fireplace side: dim, smoky, good for concealment.
- Kitchen door: behind bar.
- Back door: narrow alley to riverside.

### [[The Weighing House]]
Old merchant guild hall near market square, now Alberich office. Two storeys, clean to the point of menace. Brass weighing-scales plate on door. Smells of ink, beeswax, and faint metal.

**Key rooms for breakout:**
- **Holding cellar:** stone room, barred grate to alley/drain, one locked door, chain rings in wall.
- **Front office:** ledgers, desks, coat hooks, common lockbox.
- **Back room:** Corvin's private locked room; correspondence about Aeritch / Ashwing.
- **East door:** Corvin's habitual dusk exit.
- **Rear loading yard:** carts, crates, possible stealth route.

### [[Aldenmire]]
Crossroads town on the Greyvein river. Stone foundations, timber upper floors, market square, people with heads down. The Alberich tax post controls the bridge. Locals are frightened but not stupid; if the party causes noise, people look away to survive.

### [[The Greenward]]
Common green on north edge of town. Grass blackens from the root, pale scentless flowers, ground smells like rain without rain. A figure with no shadow may be seen from the cell window. Use this as an optional post-breakout lure.

### Merchant Guild Ruins / Pell's Hideout
Shell of the old guild building. Broken upper floor, missing windows, loose boards, excellent child-sized routes through rubble. Pell uses it as a base and knows every route between it, the Weighing House, and the river.

## Characters — Fast Descriptions and How to Play Them

### [[Corvin Fels]] — Alberich Collector
Lean, clean-shaved, expensive coat, ink-stained hands, spotless nails. Polite, precise, and always documenting. He smiles like mercy is an accounting error.

**Play him:** soft voice, never hurried, never loud. He treats violence as paperwork. He hates disorder more than danger.

**Session secret:** his Gilding is borrowed power from above. He does not fully understand it.

### Alberich Enforcers
Grey coats, brass scale badges, practical weapons, middling courage. They bully locals because locals usually fold. Against actual adventurers, their confidence degrades quickly.

**Play them:** professional until hurt; then annoyed, frightened, sloppy.

### [[Brek]] — Ground Captain
Large, calm, forgettable by design. Studded leather, scimitar, heavy crossbow. Not cruel for pleasure; dangerous because this is just logistics to him.

**Play him:** no shouting. Corrects Corvin like a supervisor walking in on a preventable workplace incident involving one dead dog and several felonies.

### [[Oswin Dray]] — Innkeeper
Broad, grey-bearded, rolled sleeves, steady hands. Kind, practical, quietly furious about Alberich control.

**Session arc:** begins as the party's safe adult; ends broken on his own floor. After the escape, he becomes emotionally damaged but not useless.

### [[Barkimedes]] — Oswin's Dog
Large, loyal, stupid in the heroic way. Tracks tension under the bar. His attack proves Corvin's concentration can be disrupted.

### [[Pell]] — Escape Vector
Fourteen-ish, wiry, quick-eyed, permanently underdressed. Knows every alley and loose fence board in Aldenmire. Wants coin, food, respect, and a way out of this town someday.

**Use him:** make him likeable fast. He is scared and furious about Barkimedes. He can bring a key, rope, guard timing, or a loose grate route.

### [[Goatius Everest]] — Secret Goat Glimpse
Unusually aware goat, always within 100m of [[Melogg]]. Seen from rooftop, alley, or guild ruins; gone when focused on. Melogg's [[Goatius's Stone]] warms.

### Faceless Figure — Greenward Seed
No shadow. Still. Watching the inn or Weighing House from the black grass. Gone when noticed. Amoris's rune aches.

## Capture and Cell Setup

**Confiscated from party:** weapons, obvious foci, armour if practical, packs, visible tools. Do **not** be too perfect — leave one path open.

**Fair hidden allowances:**
- John may keep a sliver of wire / lockpick if he beats DC 14 Sleight of Hand during capture.
- Amoris may hide a tiny item by shifting/acting if DC 14 Deception.
- Ressi may keep holy symbol if it looks decorative, DC 13 Deception/Religion argument.
- Aeritch's lyre should be confiscated and stored separately as "special property."
- Melogg cannot be fully stripped; chains are the answer.

**Cell details:**
- Stone cellar, one barred door, one high drain grate, two wall chains.
- Smells of damp stone and ink above.
- Guard outside door; second guard upstairs.
- Faint street sounds through grate.
- Window/grate line of sight can catch Greenward figure if wanted.

**Useful DCs:**
- Slip simple rope bindings: DC 13 DEX.
- Pick manacle lock with proper tools: DC 12; improvised wire: DC 15.
- Break chain from wall: DC 18 STR, advantage for Melogg if braced.
- Bend drain grate: DC 16 STR; Melogg likely can, but noise risk.
- Quietly remove grate: DC 14 Thieves' Tools / Tinker's improvisation.
- Distract guard: DC 13 Deception/Persuasion/Performance.
- Recall guard rhythm: DC 12 Perception after observing 10 minutes.
- Find hidden loose stone: DC 13 Investigation.

## Breakout Flow After Capture

### Phase 1 — Let Them Breathe and Bond

Open in the cell with silence and anger. Ask each player one quick question:
- What did you do during the walk to the Weighing House?
- What did Corvin's gold feel like after it released?
- What did your character lose when they took your gear?

Then stop narrating at them. Let them plan.

### Phase 2 — Present Three Escape Vectors

Use at least two, preferably all three:

1. **Pell at the grate:** whispers from outside, offers key/wire/route. Wants a promise: meal, coin, or protection.
2. **Guard rotation gap:** midnight shift leaves the cellar door under-watched for 3 minutes.
3. **Structural weakness:** drain grate / loose stone / Melogg-sized brute-force option, noisy but possible.

Optional complication: Aeritch is scheduled to be moved separately at dawn. This creates urgency without making the solution singular.

### Phase 3 — First Choice After Getting Out

Once they escape the cell, offer a meaningful fork:

**Option A — Sneak out immediately**
- Safest.
- Leaves gear behind unless Pell already knows where it is.
- Leads to Merchant Guild ruins / Pell's hideout.

**Option B — Recover gear from evidence room/front office**
- Moderate risk.
- Requires stealth, lockpicking, or one quick fight.
- Reward: their equipment + ledger clues.

**Option C — Break into Corvin's back room**
- Highest risk, biggest information gain.
- Finds Aeritch/Ashwing note, correspondence, barn reference, and proof Alberich are operating above local authority.

**Option D — Go back for Oswin**
- Emotionally likely.
- Oswin is alive at Tillerman's Rest, shattered, watched by one Alberich man outside.
- If they go loudly, town pressure rises; if quietly, Oswin can hide them briefly.

### Phase 4 — Immediate Post-Breakout Routes

**Route 1: Pell's Hideout / Merchant Guild Ruins**
- Best default endpoint.
- Pell offers town map, guard timings, and storage barn rumor.
- Goatius can be glimpsed on a broken upper beam watching Melogg.

**Route 2: The Tillerman's Rest**
- Emotional fallout with Oswin.
- Barkimedes' body / injury is here depending on table safety version.
- Oswin may give his hidden crossbow, cellar access, old family warning about the Greenward.

**Route 3: The Greenward**
- Mystery route.
- The faceless figure is seen again.
- Amoris's rune aches; Ressi senses root geometry; Aeritch's lyre hums if recovered.

**Route 4: Alberich Storage Barn Outside Town**
- Action route for Session 3.
- Contains confiscated goods, records, 2–3 guards, maybe an abused merchant prisoner.
- Good place to let the party win something after the scripted loss.

### Phase 5 — End Conditions

Good session endings:
- Pell turns the key and says, *"You lot want out or not?"*
- The party gets out and sees the Weighing House behind them in the rain.
- They recover one key clue from Corvin's back room and hear boots upstairs.
- They reach Oswin and he says, *"Tell me what you need."*
- They see Goatius watching from the ruins; Melogg's stone is warm.
- They see the shadowless figure on the Greenward and Amoris's hand burns.

### Easy clues
- Corvin's ledger includes party descriptions and Oswin's debt.
- Aeritch is marked separately as high-priority.
- Brek answers to the Ashwing.
- Corvin leaves by east door at dusk daily.

### Medium clues
- Guard rota shows a three-minute midnight weakness.
- A storage barn outside town holds seized goods before movement.
- Someone local is feeding Alberich information.

### Hard / risky clues
- Back room letter: *"Ravendeer asset confirmed. Do not damage instrument. Await Ashwing instruction."*
- Wax seal not Alberich: a gold-crown impression / preserved-flower scent.
- Partial map marking Greenward, Old Chord, and Weighing House with the same small symbol.

## What If They Derail?

### They try to kill Corvin before the Gilding
Let them nearly do it if the dice go wild. Then Gilding triggers early. Corvin should look genuinely shaken for half a second; that makes him better, not weaker.

### They surrender immediately
Corvin still processes them for assault / interference / Aeritch priority. Skip or shorten the opening fight, but make an enforcer shove Oswin or Barkimedes to trigger emotional resistance.

### They somehow flee the inn before capture
Brek's outer men and Corvin's Gilding catch them in the street unless their plan is excellent. If their plan is genuinely excellent, let one PC avoid capture and become the outside operator with Pell. Reward cleverness, don't defend your railroad like a nervous goblin.

### They attack Brek during transfer
Crossbows, manacles, exhaustion, and numbers. Brek does not gloat. If they create a believable opening, let them improve the later cell situation rather than fully escape.

### They refuse Pell
Use guard rotation + drain grate. Pell can remain a later contact who mutters that heroes are apparently too proud to accept competent help from short people.

### They want revenge immediately after escape
Good. Point them toward the storage barn or back room, not a direct Corvin rematch. Corvin is the knife for later; do not let them blunt it in the pantry.
