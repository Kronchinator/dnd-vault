# Session 02 — Standard Procedure

## What this session is for
Last session the party chose to stand between [[Oswin Dray|Oswin]] and the Alberich. This session shows them what that choice costs. It opens on a fight they can handle — and then on one they can't. By the end they are disarmed, grieving, and locked up, with the first crack of something far larger than a tax cartel. The goal is not to win. The goal is to make the party **hate [[Corvin Fels|Corvin]]**, plant the campaign's first seeds, and hand them a cell to break out of.

This is a **scripted defeat-and-capture**. Read the craft notes before running it.

---

## ⚠️ Table check first
This scene is built to be upsetting on purpose: the party is rendered helpless and a beloved animal is killed slowly in front of them. That is a legitimate dramatic tool and it will do exactly what you want — but only if the table has signed up for it. Your players are new. Before the session, take thirty seconds: *"This campaign goes to some dark places, including cruelty to animals and moments where your characters can't save someone. If anything ever gets to be too much, just say 'pause' and we step back, no questions."* Agree on a way to dial back in the moment (a word, a hand signal — an X-card). Having the exit available is what lets you push hard on the gas.

---

## How to run a scripted loss without losing the table
New players don't yet know what a fair fight feels like, so a loss can read as "the DM cheated." Three rules keep them with you:

1. **Let them win the first half for real.** The opening fight against the enforcers is genuinely winnable and they should drop a couple. The horror lands because they were *winning* when the rules changed.
2. **Telegraph that the new thing is beyond them — don't just declare it.** When Corvin's magic appears, describe it as alien, gold, *wrong* — something none of them has the tools to answer. The message is "this enemy is from a different story," not "you rolled badly."
3. **Agency inside the cage.** Every player still chooses their action. You narrate their failures with dignity, never contempt. And during the Barkimedes beat you hand them one real, doomed choice (below). Capture is a launchpad, not a punishment — the escape hook is live the moment the cell door shuts.

A captured party that breaks out angry is more bonded than a party that won.

## Cold open
*Pick up the morning after Session 1. Corvin gave Oswin until "tomorrow morning." It is tomorrow morning.*

> The fire in the Tillerman's Rest has burned down to coals. Grey light comes through the shutters the colour of dishwater. Oswin is up — he's been up — moving behind the bar with the careful quiet of a man trying not to wake the trouble he knows is coming. [[Barkimedes]] is under the bar, chin on his paws, ears tracking the door.
>
> Then boots. More than last night. Not trying to be quiet.

---

## Beat 1 — Corvin comes to collect
> The door opens and the cold comes in with it. Four grey-coated enforcers file in and spread along the wall like they've done it a hundred times. Last comes [[Corvin Fels|Corvin]] — coat brushed, nails clean, ledger under one arm. He does not look at the party. He sets the ledger on the bar, opens it, and smooths the page flat.
>
> "Mister Dray. The sum, plus the late assessment." A small pleasant smile. "And there's the separate matter of the disturbance on these premises last night. Property damage. Assault on Alberich personnel." He finally turns and takes in the party. "I did say I'd note your interest."

**Playing Corvin here:** unhurried, polite, faintly amused. He frames everything as paperwork. He is not here to fight — he is here to *process*. If the party negotiates or pays, he accepts coin without warmth and still itemises the "disturbance." He wants an excuse, and the party fighting back last night gave him one. He will provoke just enough to make someone swing.

**Triggering the fight:** if the party doesn't escalate, Corvin does — he nods, and an enforcer takes Oswin's ledger of patrons / starts hauling a chair toward the door "as collateral," or lays hands on Oswin again. The moment a PC moves to stop it, the enforcers draw. Let the party throw the first punch if they will; let Corvin's men throw it if they won't.

---

## Beat 2 — The fight they can win
This half is real. Run it straight and let them feel competent.

**Alberich Enforcer ×4**
- AC 12, HP 11, Speed 30 ft, Init +1
- Scimitar: +4 to hit, 1d6+2 slashing
- No special abilities; they follow orders and break formation badly under spell pressure.

**Corvin Fels (this scene)**
- AC 13, HP 27, Speed 30 ft, Init +2
- Light Crossbow: +4 to hit, 1d8+2 piercing — he prefers to stay back and *direct*
- STR 8 (−1). **Remember this.** Corvin is physically weak; it matters horribly in Beat 6.
- He does not melee if he can help it. He gives orders, marks the ledger, and watches.

Let the party drop one or two enforcers. Let [[Melogg]] be a wall, let [[Aeritch Ravendeer|Aeritch]] or [[Anakis]] land a spell, let [[John Jackbox|John]] get a sneak attack off the bar. Let it feel good. Corvin's expression sours — not fear, *distaste.* Disorder offends him.

**Then, when the party is up and an enforcer or two is down, Corvin decides the disorder has gone on long enough.**

---

## Beat 3 — The Gilding *(set-piece — scripted)*
> Corvin sighs, the way a man sighs at a stain. He closes the ledger. He pulls off one glove, finger by finger, and lays his bare hand flat on the bar.
>
> The wood **gilds**. Gold runs out from under his palm in fine veins, like cracks filling with molten light, and the air goes thick and sweet — resin, sap, something floral and wrong. The gold leaves the bar. It finds you. It crawls up boots and wrists and throats and **hardens**, clear and amber and shot through with gold, until every one of you is held like an insect set in stone.

**This holds the whole party. It is not a save-or-suck you can muscle out of — it is the story telling them they have met something from a different shelf.** If a player insists on rolling, let them (Strength save, DC 18+, or just "you strain and the amber only tightens"). Describe the binding as *beyond* arcane — none of them recognises it, including the casters.

**Gilded Binding (Corvin's borrowed gift) — for your reference**
- Golden amber light that hardens around limbs; restrains all hostile creatures in the room.
- **Concentration.** Corvin must hold it. (This is the hinge for Beat 4.)
- He can speak and walk while concentrating; he cannot fight meaningfully while holding it (he won't need to).
- It is *borrowed.* Corvin did not earn this and does not fully understand it — play a flicker of something almost like reverence, or fear, when he uses it. He calls it nothing. He just uses it like a key he was handed. (Source: the Architect / [[The Hollow Crown|Gilded King]]. The party must not learn this. See seeds.)

**Seeds to fire in this exact moment (quietly, no explanation):**
- **[[Amoris Scilia|Amoris]]:** the brand on her hand *flares* — heat, almost a pull, as the gold spreads. It has never answered another person's magic before. Pass her a note. Do not explain.
- **[[Ressi Res Essiressires|Ressi]]:** as she strains against the amber and instinctively reaches for power, her face **slips** — a cheek slackens, henna-script flickers under the skin — then settles. One other player should glimpse it.
- The smell — resin, preserved-flower — is the same note that will hang around the [[The Old Chord]] shrine and around [[Kallista's Brooch]]. Land it on the senses now; pay it off later.

> Corvin flexes his fingers. The gold answers. He looks around the room at six people held like specimens and his pleasant smile finally reaches his eyes for the first time. "There," he says softly. "Order."

---

## Beat 4 — Barkimedes
> You hear him before you see him — a low building sound from under the bar that isn't a growl so much as a decision. Then [[Barkimedes]] comes over the bar in one ugly heave, all head and shoulders and no plan, and goes straight for the man making the bad smell.
>
> Corvin's concentration **stumbles.** The gold flickers — for half a heartbeat the amber on all of you goes soft as warm honey —

**Hand the party their one doomed beat.** The flicker loosens the bonds for an instant. Go round fast: *"For one second, one of you could move a hand, say a word, reach for something — what do you do?"* Let them choose who and what. Make it cost-free to attempt and impossible to win with. A freed hand gets grabbed again. A thrown dagger goes wide as the gold re-sets. A shouted word reaches Oswin. **Whatever they spend it on, narrate it landing emotionally and failing mechanically — then the amber hardens again, tighter.**

> — and then Corvin's boot catches Barkimedes across the jaw and the dog goes down hard. Two enforcers are on him before he can rise, pinning the big stupid loyal head to the flagstones. The gold sets again, harder than before. Corvin straightens his cuff.

---

## Beat 5 — Oswin
> The kitchen door bangs. Oswin is there, and the steadiness the party has only ever seen on him is **gone.** He takes in the room — the gold, the patrons set in it like flies, his dog pinned and bleeding — and something in him just folds. He comes around the bar and he gets down. On his knees. On the floor of his own inn.
>
> "Please." His voice is wrecked. "Please — let the patrons go. They're good people. They didn't — it was my fault, the trouble, all of it. I'll get the money. I'll get it, I swear to every god, just — let them go. Let my dog up. *Please.*"

Let this sit. Let a player or two have a line if they can get a word past the amber. Don't rush Oswin's begging — its length is the point. The party has only seen this man kind and unbreakable. Break him.

---

## Beat 6 — What Corvin does
This is the scene the whole session is built around. Run it slow, run it with dignity for the victim, and do not flinch.

> Corvin looks at the kneeling man for a long moment. And then he **laughs.** Not cruelly, exactly — *delightedly*, like Oswin has done something genuinely funny. He wipes the corner of his eye.
>
> "Gods, you people," he says. "You *grovel.* It's extraordinary. You crawl on the floor of the building you'll lose anyway and you say *please* to the man holding the deed." He shakes his head, still smiling. "Very well then. Since you asked so nicely."
>
> He turns to his men. "Kill the dog."

**Let the party try.** They will strain, shout, threaten, beg, attempt to cast. The amber holds. Narrate each attempt as real and as failing — *"the gold drinks the sound of it,"* *"you feel the word die in your throat,"* *"you can see Melogg's whole frame shaking against it and the amber doesn't even crack."* This is the cost of helplessness and the players need to feel every inch of it.

> But the enforcer hesitates — it's a *dog* — and Corvin makes a small, irritated sound, like a man whose subordinate has fumbled a simple form. "Must I do everything." He takes the enforcer's sword. He is not a strong man and the blade is heavier than he's used to; you can see him adjust his grip.
>
> He does it himself. Slowly. It takes him more than one stroke — his arm isn't built for it — and he keeps going with the same patient, unbothered focus he gives a ledger, until it's done. Then he hands the sword back, hilt-first, and wipes his palm on a cloth from his coat.

**Craft notes for this moment:**
- Corvin's **low STR is the horror**, not an obstacle. It means the killing is clumsy and prolonged and he does it anyway, calmly, because the difficulty simply doesn't register as a reason to stop. That is far more chilling than competence.
- He kills the dog and not the party **on purpose, in-fiction:** the party are *assets* (Aeritch is flagged high-priority in his ledger; the rest are leverage and witnesses). The dog is worth nothing to anyone but the people he's humiliating. He destroys the only thing in the room that has no value to him, precisely because they love it. Cold, not hot. He never raises his voice.
- Keep it spare on the page. You don't need gore. You need the *calm.* Let the silence after do the work.

> Corvin looks around the gilded, silent room, mildly satisfied, like a man who has tidied a desk.

---

## Beat 7 — Brek puts the leash on
*Corvin has overstepped — not morally, in his world, but operationally. He's made noise, made a mess, and indulged himself on company time. His captain is not pleased.*

> The door opens one more time. The big calm man from last night — [[Brek]] — steps in, takes in the scene in a single sweep, and his face does something tired. "Fels." Flat. "What is this."
>
> Corvin opens his mouth. Brek raises a hand. "Pack it up. The elf's flagged — that means *she* goes nowhere and *you* touch nothing until the Ashwing's word comes down. You know that." He looks at the gilded party, and at the dog, and back at Corvin, and whatever he thinks he keeps off his face. "Get them off the floor and put them somewhere they'll keep."

**Brek is not a rescuer.** He's a professional annoyed by an amateur's indulgence. But the party will clock that *Corvin answers to someone*, and that even Corvin's casual cruelty made a hardened enforcer flinch. File that.

> The gold lets go all at once — you drop, pins and needles, the sweet resin smell fading. Before anyone can use the freedom, there are crossbows up and Brek's men are moving with the bored efficiency of people who do this for a living. You're disarmed. Hands bound. Walked out into the grey morning.

**Where they're held:** the [[The Weighing House]] holding room, or the Tillerman's own cellar repurposed as a cell — your call. Guards at every entrance. Aeritch is noted as separate and special (don't let the party miss that she's being treated differently). Oswin is left kneeling on his own floor.

---

## The rest of the session — getting agency back fast
Don't let the helplessness linger past the capture. The instant the cell door shuts, the party should be *doing* again.

- **The cell.** Bound, disarmed, grieving, furious. Give them room to talk — this is where the party truly forms, in the dark, angry together. Let them plan. Let Melogg test the walls (he's Large and strong — the guards know it, hence the chains). Let John work the bindings (Thieves' Tools expertise — if his kit wasn't confiscated, or improvise). Let the casters realise what's drained and what isn't.
- **[[Pell]] arrives.** The kid saw the whole thing from the guild ruins. He's frightened and he's furious about the dog too, and he's exactly small enough to get where guards don't look. He becomes the **escape vector** — a lifted key, a loose grate, a guard rotation he's memorised (*"Corvin leaves by the east door at dusk, every day"*). Price as usual: coin, a meal, or being treated like he matters. This is his introduction; make him likeable fast.
- **Almost-Goatius.** During the march to the cell or through the cell's high window, [[Melogg]] half-notices a goat watching from a rooftop / the guild ruins — gone the instant he focuses. His stone is warm. Don't explain. (See [[Goatius Everest]].)
- **The Greenward seed.** If the holding room faces north, the cell's window looks out on [[The Greenward]] — and at the edge of the black grass, in the grey dawn, a figure stands with no shadow, facing the inn, before it's gone. Amoris's brand aches when she looks at it. (See [[The Seal of Thoria]].)

**End on the break-out in motion, or on the cusp of it** — the key in the lock, the guard turning, Pell's face at the grate. Pick up next session with the escape.

## What the party knows now
- Corvin has access to magic far beyond a tax collector — gold that hardens and binds. Someone gave it to him.
- Corvin is not just cold; he is cruel, and he will indulge that cruelty when it costs him nothing.
- Corvin answers to Brek, and Brek answers to the Ashwing, and Aeritch is wanted by name from the top.
- Oswin is broken and his dog is dead, and the party watched and could not stop it.

## XP / pacing
- Award XP for the opening fight (the enforcers they dropped) and a milestone bump for surviving the set-piece and the capture — they earned levels through story, not kills, this session. Suggested: bring the lower-level PCs up toward Kael's level 3 over Sessions 2–3 so the party is even before the escape arc.
